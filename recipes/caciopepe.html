How to Cook Cacio e Pepe

Cacio e Pepe is a classic Italian pasta dish that translates to "cheese and pepper." It's simple yet incredibly delicious. Here's how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz (225g) spaghetti or bucatini
  • 1 cup (100g) freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the pasta and cook according to package instructions until al dente.
  3. While the pasta is cooking, in a large skillet, toast the black pepper over medium heat for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta and transfer it to the skillet with the toasted pepper.
  5. Add about 1/2 cup of the reserved pasta water to the skillet and turn the heat to low.
  6. Add the grated Pecorino Romano cheese to the skillet and quickly toss everything together with tongs until the cheese melts and coats the pasta, adding more pasta water if necessary to create a creamy sauce.
  7. Season with salt to taste.
  8. Divide the pasta among serving plates, sprinkle with additional black pepper if desired, and serve immediately.

Enjoy your homemade Cacio e Pepe!
